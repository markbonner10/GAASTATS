Croke Park
On this page, you can find information about Croke Park. Croke Park is the home of the GAA and is located in Dublin, Ireland.It has a capacity of over 80,000 spectators. First opened in 1884, Croke Park has undergone several renovations and expansions over the years. It is the venue for major GAA events, including the All-Ireland Senior Football and Hurling Championships, as well as other sports and concerts. Croke Park is also one of the most iconic stadiums in Ireland and is known for its rich history and passionate atmosphere during GAA matches. It is a symbol of Irish culture and sportsmanship, attracting fans from all over the world to experience the excitement of Gaelic games. Sitting in the heart of Dublin, Croke Park is one of Ireland's most visted landmarks, with its guided tours, museum and events.Croke Park is not as well known around the world as the Aviva due to the Rule 42 from the GAA which prohibts foreig sports being played at GAA stadiums and picthes.This rule is sometimes lifted in order to allow for more revenue to be generated for the GAA and local economy.
