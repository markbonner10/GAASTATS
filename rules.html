New Rules for 2024

Sin Bin: A new disciplinary rule was introduced where players who commit certain fouls or misconduct can be temporarily sent to the "sin bin" for a specified period of time. This adds a new dimension to the game and encourages fair play. 3.Handing ball back to a fouled player.This rule was introudced to allow for a more disciplined and fair game. This adds an extra layer of strategy and excitement to the game. 5.3 up 3 down rule: This rule was introduced to encourage more attacking play and increase the number of scoring opportunities. These new rules have been implemented to make the game more exciting and engaging for players and fans alike, while also promoting fair play and sportsmanship.
Fundamental Rules of the GAA
The GAA has a set of fundamental rules that govern the game and ensure fair play 1.Maximum number of players: Each team is allowed a maximum of 15 players on the field at any given time, including the goalkeeper. 2. Scoring: In Gaelic football, a point is scored by kicking or punching the ball over the crossbar and between the goalposts, while a goal is scored by kicking or punching the ball into the net below the crossbar. In hurling, a point is scored by hitting the ball over the crossbar and between the goalposts, while a goal is scored by hitting the ball into the net below the crossbar. 3. Match duration: A standard GAA match consists of two halves, each lasting 30 minutes for senior inter-county matches, and 25 minutes for club matches. There is a halftime break of 10 minutes. 4.Players must only take 4 steps with the ball in their hand before they must either bounce it, solo it (kick it back into their hand), or pass it to a teammate. This rule encourages skillful play and promotes teamwork. 5. In tackling,players must not use a closed fist or strike an opponent with their hand. Instead, they must use an open hand to dispossess the opponent of the ball. This rule promotes fair play and player safety.
